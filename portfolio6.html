University of Melbourne
Redesigning the Classroom Experience





The Product Management class at the University of Melbourne is a weekly
two hour lecture broken up in half: one hour taught by the course professor and one hour taught by industry guests from Accenture Interactive. An additional one hour tutorial class is added to give students a smaller class setting where they participate and apply the knowledge they’ve learned in class. With the course encompassing three different learning environments with different professors, the University of Melbourne wanted students to redesign the course to create a more enriching and engaging experience for future students.


Problem Statement



Research Process
I conducted a student survey as a part of my primary research in order to better understand the target segment. I administered surveys to the tutorial classes and was able to conclude average student opinions and behaviours. Then I formed possible hypothesis of explanation and conducted interviews with a number of survey respondents to gain further insights.


Problem Analysis

1. Lecture Design
I observed an average attendance of 15-30 students (out of 153) every week after Week 3 of the twelve-week semester. The survey found that 80% of respondents are frequent users of lecture recordings and a majority use it as a substitution for attending lectures.

2. Content Structure
More than 30% of respondents did not find the lecture content to be well structured, while 30% remained neutral. From interviews with some of these students, a frequent complaint was the size of learning content and the long hours of lecture.

3. Lack of Differentiation & Consequences
Interviewees found a minimal difference between attending lectures and lecture recording in terms of learning quality. Since lecture participation doesn’t affect final marks, most would opt to fast-forward through the lecture online at their own convenience.

4. Anonyminity
A lecture of 153 students in a large-size lecture hall hardly allows the chance for students to know each other. The anonymity then permits disengagement and absenteeism.


5. Absorption & Understanding
Survey respondents had given Product Management an average mark of 66 out of 100 compared to other marketing subjects they have completed before. Despite their motivation to learn, a majority of interviewees found the learning content hard to absorb due to its difficulty and size, thus they felt that they did not have a solid grasp on the material.

6. Case Application
Despite the frequent sessions with our current industry presenter, 50% of respondents agree they would like even more real life examples and experiences. Interviewees explained that they felt a gap between the lecture content and the presentation content.


Consumer Archetype
This is the behavioural profile of an average student enrolled in the course:

Emotional: Aside from getting course credit, Emily is taking Product Management because she was interested by the course description and subject content, wanting a class with real life applications and speakers.

Cognitive: Emily is curious about the different fields of marketing, and she is motivated to gain a better understanding of marketing before graduation and learn how she can apply her studies to real cases.


User Journey



Product Concepts

1. Two-in-One Seminars:
A proposal is to shorten the lecture time to one hour, and follow it with a thirty minute tutorial directly afterwards. In total, there will be two seminars each a week. By dividing the lecture into two, the learning content will also be divided into smaller, more absorbable units. The tutorial that follows would then allow instantaneous application of knowledge while they are still fresh.

However, if students do not attend the lecture beforehand, they will not get the most out of tutorials and the knowledge of their tutors, thus giving them more incentives to attend lectures.


2. Team Points:
A team-based participation mark will be introduced during the lecture. Below is the new assessment tasks and corresponding weight of our new product. Students will be randomly allocated into 1 of 3 teams upon enrolment. A participation mark will be weekly given to each team based on the number and quality of their answers asked during that week’s lecture.




3. Lectures Before Presentations
Industry presenters will come and make the presentation in Seminar 2. By arranging industry presenters after the first lecture & tutorial, students can have better absorption and deeper understanding of the weekly contents. The industry presentation links the theories learned with real-life applications.


New User Journey
Gap Analysis
The gap analysis identifies two headings that will help reflect the ideal
future state of the course:


1. Participation-oriented vs Exam-oriented
2. High industry engagement vs Low industry engagement
Product Management has been positioned with three 3rd level marketing subjects - Advertising and Promotion, Service and Relationship Marketing and Digital Marketing. These three subjects are major competitors to Product Management.

As shown in the archetype chart, 14% of respondents have studied Advertising and Promotion, which is the most popular subject amongst other 3rd year marketing subjects. Even though A&P is a highly exam-oriented subject, the highly engaging industry content and presenters within its lectures has been one of the key touchpoint for students.

Currently, Product Management is lying at the second quadrant of the gap analysis as paper based assessments and exam take up to 75% of total marks of this subject. Our ideal future position is to position the subject into the first quadrant with our new product concept. We will focus more on team-based participation which will account more weight for the total marks and more engagement with industry presenters.




Value Proposition



Risk Management



Most of these risks are applicable to the current classroom settings, so there is no stark difference in risks for the new course implementation. Before the three new ideas of the plan are launched and tested for incoming Product Management students, a risk assessment should reduce any negative consequences.

The engagement and stimulation between students and teaching staffs should be continually monitored and each stage of new product development is important to successfully launch the new and revised course structure.
