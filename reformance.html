Fjord: Accenture Interactive






At Fjord, my teammate Monique and I came up with an immersive experience for the future of musical storytelling. Using the design research framework to guide the user experience, we were able to reimagine the space of musical performances by creating a data-driven and technologically relevant concert experience. With feedback and guidance from Fjord’s designers and directors, we pitched The Reformance.

Design Framework

2. Empathize: User Interviews

We interviewed 18 - 24 year olds, combed through music chat rooms, and read academic papers  to learn the painpoints individuals have with modern music experiences. Three takeaways are:

- Interpersonal: People use music as a tool to bond with others, the artist, and the artist’s story .

- Introspective: Music alter or reflects people's moods. Big productions & constant promotion distract from the intimacy of going to a performance

- Cathartic: Music can be a great medium to relieve stress, find relief and relaxation, release any tension or emotion

3. Define: Problem Statement

Based on user interviews, we decided on three themes to improve the concert experience. First, we will reformat the spatial aspects, since space guides the timing of what users witness and interact with. Second, are the visuals, which associates content with outside influencers and unseen factors. Lastly, we will introduce haptics, so users are able to touch, hold, and feel at physical and digital touchpoints.


4. Ideate: Our Product





Musician Spotlight: Janelle Monae
Our immersive gallery performance will feature singer Janelle Monae, a dynamic storyteller who creates songs that deal with topics about art & technology and her identity as a black queer woman. We thought this gallery would fit her latest album “Dirty Computer” because of its reference to technology, empowerment, and community.




User Experience: A Walkthrough




Entrance:
- Guests check in through a QR code on Spotify 
- The Reformance collects users' streaming data to customize experience











Paths:
Guests are directed to either Path 1 or Path 2 based on the data captured.

Path 1: For those curious in the music making process
- Voice recognition & augmented reality collects users' responses to the prompt:
Music is ___?

- Speaking into enabled devices  converts speech into text, then visually renders in space 

- Everyone can see or learn something from each other’s answers






Path 2: For those who want to know about the artist
- Voice recognition & augmented reality collects users' responses to the prompt:
What Makes You Proud?


- Users point their phone around the room with an AR-enabled camera through Spotify to view everyone’s responses

- Can also use audio devices to ask Janelle questions that she will answer during the performance


Performance:
- Users end up in a 360° performance space and are a part of the show

- The questions and discussion from the pathway will guide the flow of dialogue from Monae

- Utilizes AR goggles to visualize the messages across the room









End of Performance
- After the musical performance which is immersive & communicative, listeners are left with multiple impressions and takeaways.

- Bonus: Guests receive a surprise song or audio clip on Spotify from Monae as a thank you .
