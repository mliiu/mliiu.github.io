Which A24 Film Are You by Location?





I enjoy watching A24 films because all of their movies are able to perfectly encapsulate the feeling of a location in its storytelling. I've created an interactive map documenting where A24 films are popular in the U.S. based on Google Trend searches and demographics during the films' domestic release dates. Sidenote: As someone who grew up and went to school where Lady Bird (2017) was mainly filmed (not as much in Sacramento as you would be tricked to believe), I am a bit salty it did not claim LA.








Geographic Map of A24 Films
Based on Google Search Trends, from West Coast to East Coast.

Most Dense Least Dense




Ex Machina (2014)

🌳 🤖 🌳

Most Popular Search Location: Seattle, WA

Genre: Sci-Fi

Filmed in Norway, this Sci-Fi thriller had a higher interest in the West Coast in Washington, Oregon, and Central California (Monterey, SLO, Santa Barbara)


Slow West (2015)

🐴 🤠 🌾

Most Popular Search Locations: Spokane, WA

Genre: Western

Set in Colorado plains, this film was actually shot in South Island, New Zealand (Mount Cook Area!). Popular in the states of Washington, Colorado, and Utah for it's Western appeal.

Midsommar (2019)

💐 🌼 👯 🐻 🔥 👸 🌸 💐

Most Popular Search Location: Portland, OR

Genre: Horror

While popular throughout the States, this festive horror is set in Sweden but filmed in Budapest, Hungary. This film had higher interests in most of Oregon, Los Angeles, and Austin.

Lean on Pete (2017)

🧢 👦🏻 🐴

Most Popular Search Location: Bend, OR

Genre: Western

This local indie film starring a horse, boy, and Steve Buscemi was filmed in the Portland Meadows. No surprise most popular in Oregon.

An ode to the suburbs of Sacramento, the film has the highest interest in Northern CA. Also struck a chord in East Coast states (MA, NY, VT) for the liberal arts college appeal.
