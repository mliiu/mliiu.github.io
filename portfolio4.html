Ethical Fashion Visualization

Ethical fashion represents an approach to the design, sourcing, and manufacture of clothing which maximizes benefits to people and communities while minimizing impact on the environment. I was inspired to create an informational and visual data clustering of fashion companies after reading a yearly report on ethics and practices in Australia/New Zealand’s fashion industry.

Problem
Fashion companies are often not transparent about their practices in sustainability. I wanted to present a consolidated visualization map that will enable users to look into fast fashion ethics and become informed about companies’ recent practices and relations to other brands under the same name.


Visualization

Data Resource
I created a visualization of fashion companies and their brands by referring to an Excel spreadsheet, which I sourced from the 2017 Ethical Fashion Report compiled by Tearfund.org.nz. Tearfund based its qualifications of how ethical a fashion company is by looking at 7 encompassing factors:

1. Overall Grade: Average sum of the below criteria

2. Policies: Code of conduct and policies meet human rights standards. Is there freedom of movement for employees to leave and enter work voluntarily, protection of worker's health and safety, and is this applied down the supply chain?

3. Knowing your Suppliers: To what degree in supply chain traced? Is there the ability to trace the location of suppliers and is there a supplier list available to the public for transparency?

4. Auditing & Supplier Relationships: Raw Materials/Final Manufacturing/Input Production: Are suppliers' factories audited and public? How are the working conditions for suppliers?

5. Worker Empowerment: Raw Materials/Final Manufacturing/Input Production: Is there access to unions and collective bargaining? Are living wages paid to workers? Is there freedom of association and child labor policies?

7. Living Wages: Raw Materials/Final Manufacturing/Inputs Production: Are there iniatives to improve wages, is it a livable wage, and is there a fair trade initiative?

Each company receives a grading for their efforts in each of these 7 categories on a letter scale of A to F. You can get a more granular sense of what classifies the grade scaling in Tearfund’s analysis research here.

Informing about Company Ethics





By clustering major fashion companies with their subsidiary brands, individuals can be more informed about the impact created by these corporations.

For example, when you click to view a node, a information guide appears about the company’s ethical transparency and its scale rating for each category.
